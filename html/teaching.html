Teaching activities

I am passionate about teaching and learning, and about harnessing the power of new technologies to provide better higher education in science, technology, engineering and mathematics.

2020, EPFL

Head teaching assistant for MICRO 511 Image Processing I and MICRO 512 Image Processing II at EPFL
My main teaching task at EPFL has been to lead the development of "Remote and Interactive image processing programming laboratories with Jupyter" for the image processing courses taught by Prof. Michael Unser and Prof. Dimitri Van De Ville. The EPFL has prepared a nice article about our development: Remote practical labs with personalized feedback. Here below you can also find a video made by EPFL to illustrate our project and an academic talk where I describe it in detail.

2014-2018, KTH

Teaching assistant for EQ2300 Digital Signal Processing

The bulk of my teaching activities during my Ph.D. was serving as a teaching assistant each fall semester. In total, I guided students through approximately 100 hours of exercise sessions and 20 hours of laboratory sessions, and I lectured for 4 hours. I also took care of the development of new course material for exercise sessions by restructuring the entire exercise collection to bring together the content in the lectures and the exercises.

Supervision of student theses

I regularly supervise M.Sc. and B.Sc. theses, as well as semester and student projects, in a variety of topics related to my research interests.
