I am passionate about teaching and learning, and about harnessing the power of new technologies to provide better higher education in science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM).

2014-2018, KTH, Teaching assistant, EQ2300 Digital Signal Processing

The bulk of my teaching activities during my Ph.D. was serving as a teaching assistant each fall semester. In total, I guided students through approximately 100 hours of exercise sessions and 20 hours of laboratory sessions, and I lectured for 4 hours. I also took care of the development of new course material for exercise sessions by restructuring the entire exercise collection to bring together the content in the lectures and the exercises.
