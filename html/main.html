Research interests

My research spans topics in signal processing and its applications, machine learning, inverse problems, mathematical statistics, and optimization. In general, I am drawn to engineering problems that require an extensive applied mathematics toolbox.

I strive to make the complete link between an applied engineering solution and rather abstract mathematical results. These last years, my focus has been on inverse problems for image reconstruction. During this time, I have studied practical problems in positron emission tomography, the stability of image-reconstruction algorithms, and multidimensional probability density estimation techniques based on regular splines. Previously, I have been involved in projects concerning wireless communications and the foundations of privacy-preserving data analysis.
